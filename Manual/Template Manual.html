On the Subject of Time Signatures

Your flavor text should go here.

  • The modules have two displays. The top one will cycle the bottom display,and vise versa.
  • One of the numbers in the displays will be colored red. Pressing the top display on this number will produce a sequence of ten measures, each with a consistant pulse and tempo, but a not so consistant time signature.
  • When a measure is played, the first note will always be emphasized with a higher pitch and volume. The module will always be playing eight notes, but the time signature is signaled using emphasized notes with a louder volume.
  • Another number colored green will be on any of the displays. Submit all ten time signatures in order by holding the display with the number that is colored green.
  • A strike or a press the red display will cause the module to change the location of the green and red numbers.

Time Signatues

  • A quarter note (4) is twice the length of an eigth note (8). A half note (2) is twice the length of a quarter note (8).
